An optical float for plastic detection and tracking - CAPTAIN

Marine plastic pollution is a major environmental threat, with millions of tons of plastic waste entering oceans annually. These plastics break down into microplastics and release toxic chemicals, which harm marine life and enter the human food chain. Sources include land-based activities like littering and maritime activities like shipping. Despite efforts like the EU Plastic Strategy and the US EPA's initiatives, pollution persists, particularly in regions like the Mediterranean Sea. Traditional monitoring methods are limited, but satellite-based observations show promise, despite sensitivity challenges. Within the framework of the joint IEEE GRSS/Van Allen Foundation Student Grand Challenge, the CAPTAIN project introduces a cost-effective solution using Lagrangian buoys with plastic detection capabilities and satellite connectivity to track and mitigate marine plastic pollution in real-time.

CAPTAIN bouy is currently moored offshore Barcelona for a long-term test.
